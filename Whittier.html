Mt Whittier , Washington – September 23, 2007

Almost exactly a year ago I hiked a part of the Mt Margaret Backcountry loop near Mt St Helens and passed the Mt Whittier trail junction with a "Danger/Warning" sign marking the start.  It wasn't my planned destination but I promised myself I'd revisit it in the future.  Very little info of the Mt Whittier trail exists on the web but it has an unsual high rate of mentioning extreme danger when info is found.  Coming off the thrill and disappointment of my Broken Top trip, I had forgotten all about my Mt Whittier promise.  On a cool and cloudy Saturday, I suddenly remembered my Mt Whittier plans so I got the OK from my housemates to make it my Sunday adventure.

A 3am departure and 150 miles later got me to the Norway Pass trailhead by 5am to start the long trek in the dark.  The approach, sights, sounds, and weather was almost indentical to my visit the same time last year including a few very close suprise encounters with some lone elks.  By the time I got to the Mt Whittier trail junction, the cloud ceiling was oscillating at my elevation which meant the Mt Whittier peak would not be visible.

The Mt Whittier ridge bisects a large area of elk and mountain goat habitat.  The elk like the large meadows and the goats like the steep terrain.  Both find protection due to the area's remoteness.  Both species travel over Mt Whittier's ridge at its low points.  This low point seemed to be a popular pass as elk and goat tracks were everywhere as you can see in the bottom of this picture:

Returning I had a chance to give a second look at some of the dicier slope cuts.  Here's one that was partcularly troublesome.  It was a steep rock slope cut that was filled with mud and rock and no margin of error in case of a slip.  On my way back I did a little trail cleanup for the next guy.  The trail proceeds from the lower right downhill to the green patch on the right and then uphill on the dark straight diagonal line to the flat part in the upper left.

AN IMPORTANT NOTE: Continuing on the Mt Whittier ridge will take you down to the Green River Valley on the outskirts of the Mt St Helens National Volcanic Monument.  It's near there that irresponsible government agencies want to grant a mining permit for a 3000 acre copper mine.  Read all about it at links found through Google.  The establishment of any mining operation would be a huge irreversible tragedy in this area.  It's another clear sign that the Bush attitude has permeated all levels of government.  If you have a chance to vote, please vote in the direction that would prevent things like this copper mine.
