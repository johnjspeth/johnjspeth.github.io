Annual Mt St Helens Autumn Revisit, Washington – November 4, 2007

Pulling off my annual Mt St Helens autumn hike was in jeopardy this year because winter made a very early arrival with big snow in the mountains starting in late September.  The risk was that the roads would snow in early.  A long dry spell in October melted all the early snow and the roads stayed open long enough for me to sneak in my trip starting at Climber's Bivouac.  The recent changeable weather would make a trip this day iffy but I knew I'd get some interesting clouds if I could just get high enough.

Due to some thoughtful pre-planning and packing, I was able to leave the house by 3am and hit the trail by 4:30.  The trail was rock solid frozen and that made for some fast traveling conditions up to the tree line.  It was still dark when I arrived at the tree line and I started to get my first wind warnings from Mother Nature.  Despite the building wind, it wasn't enough to chase me home.  My early arrival gave me great opportunities for sunrise/cloud pictures.
