Broken Top, Oregon – September 13, 2007

Broken Top is the “fourth Sister” of the Three Sisters in central Oregon.  It’s the craggy and crumbly 9000 ft neighbor to South Sister.  I recently learned that climbing one of its’ two prominent peaks might be non-technical so I decided to see if I had the stuff to make it.

I followed the usual overnight trip prototype: camp in my car at the trailhead parking lot the night before and get an early start.  After mis-setting my alarm wristwatch I finally arose an hour late to make a 5am start.  I hiked along Fall Creek under starlight for about 3 miles before I finally got good enough light to enjoy the constant revelation of noisy waterfalls and cascades.  As I returned, I realized how much I had missed.

As I approached the top, verticality started to dominate my view.  The ridgeline trail became narrow and winding.  Here's a view looking down to the old moraines on Broken Top's north side:

Broken Top has at least two peaks, all of which are steep and craggy.  Here's a view of the lesser of two peaks visible from my approach:

Some gendarmes along the “trail”:

From my lunch bench near the top, I had the time to study a glacier on the north side and the colorful rock layers (center in the distance).  The glacier looks like a snowy slope but it's a solid block of ice.

Another view down from my lunch perch:

And finally, the peak, just about 50 more feet more to go.  It would have been an easy scramble over rocks but, sadly, I didn't do it.  One slip and I would have surely tumbled to my death.  This is the point where most technicians will rope up.  Others try to make it alone.  All I could think of was "What would I be thinking about just before my head is split open as I fall?"  I didn't want to think any more about it.  I sulked and then started down.
