Ape Canyon – June 19, 2005

The Ape Canyon trail is on a ribbon of untouched old-growth forest that escaped the 1980 blast and subsequent lahar (mud- and other stuff-slide) devastation.  Where the tree’s finally end, you find yourself at the top of Ape Canyon with views that are unobstructed by trees including the Plains of Abraham.

As usual I started in the dark.  Here’s my first view: Mt Adams at 7am.

Mt St Helens is constantly puffing steam and oozing lava. Here’s a typical puff.

Here’s another view through some dead trees on the edge of the forest.

Here’s the upper notch of Ape Canyon.

Here’s looking across the Plains of Abraham. The Plains of Abraham is a huge expanse of lahar deposition with no large features – kind of biblical.  It’s about two miles from where I was standing to the hill on the left.  Mt St Helens climbs straight up just out of the picture to the left.

The trail is closed beyond due to concern of volcanic activity.  So I climbed a nearby hill overlooking Ape Canyon and watched a herd of elk sunning themselves while I ate lunch.
