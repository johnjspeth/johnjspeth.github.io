Munra Point in the Columbia River Gorge, Oregon - June 2003

We just got back from a long weekend in pleasant Camp Sherman on the dry side of the Cascades. Nick has developed a slight distaste for our hikes because he's bored by the long drives and monotonous rides on Dad's back. So I didn't get my daily hikes like I wanted to. I did however discover a neat summer solstice activity: sunset hiking. I made two hikes up Black Butte in the setting sun. It's amazing how much light the sun gives at 10pm in late June. But that's another story.

After the weekend, we got home around noon and I was itching to burn off some energy so I was cut loose for a few hours. It was cloudy and cool and it looked like the perfect opportunity to try Munra Point in the Gorge. It's a short but sweet vertical scramble. It's only 45 minutes from home and about a mile up. Like most Gorge hikes, it starts very close to the roar of I-84. This trail was one of the noisiest but it ascends so quickly it wasn't so bad.

About half way up, the expansive Gorge views started to open up.

The Gorge is always full of surprises if you look for them. Under a nice shady oak tree I found a patch of wild lillies. My little camera wonderfully reproduced what I found. These lillies are ripe as ripe can be.

Here's the "top" in the upper left. It's probably best to turn back if it's too windy or raining. Fortunately it was neither. So I made my way up.

To my right was the start of the ridgeline that carries the trail into the mountains.

Here's a picture of where I was standing when I took the previous two pictures. It was a little tricky to shoot pictures and mind my feet. There wasn't much to slow me down in case of a misstep. That's the Columbia River down there.

The top was fun for a few minutes but the ridgeline seemed like it needed exploring. The next four pictures show my knife-edge walk for a short distance.

I stopped here to turn around. It was tough to say no to the little voice in my head telling me to "go just a little bit more". I had to go home.

Munra Point is a great hike and I can't wait to return to hike out to as far as I can. I've read there's a large waterfall back there somewhere. I caught a glimpse of it from the Point.
