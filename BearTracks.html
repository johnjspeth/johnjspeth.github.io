Bear Tracks in the Snow on Lambertson Butte near Mt Hood – October 30, 2005

It looked like winter was on an early arrival schedule this year so I figured I should get a final low-snow run up a place on Mt Hood I’ve never been before.  I picked the cliffy Lambertson Butte area.  I figured there’d be a little snow but nothing I needed to be super prepared for.  I was more worried about the rain that they forecast for the day.  Near my destination, the butte peak, I was annoyed to see tracks wandering around in the four new inches of snow.  I thought I was the only one up there so early in the morning.  I got a closer look and could see they were bear tracks.  Bear signs are rare on Mt Hood (at least for my untrained eye) so this was a real treat.  I figured the tracks were at most a few hours old because the snowfall had stopped sometime in the night.

It was 9am and low-light snow pictures are hard to get.  Here’s a good bear print: slightly smaller than my size 8 boot.  He must be a size 7 bear.

Here’s a better picture for scale.

In the shallow snow under the trees, I found some nice tracks that showed toenails.  They looked sharp and long.  Prior to finding those tracks I thought I might even have some luck seeing the bear.  In fact, the area I was in was cliffs on two sides and his tracks were headed straight for the intersection of both cliffs.  The toenails reminded me that I should let the bear be so I dropped my fantasy, made some noise to announce my arrival, and continued to the peak.

On my return I never saw any new tracks.  Bear was probably long gone even before I saw his tracks.
